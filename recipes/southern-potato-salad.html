Southern Potato Salad

Description

Southern potato salad is a beloved classic that embodies the essence of comfort food with its creamy, tangy, and slightly sweet flavors. This traditional dish showcases tender, boiled potatoes as the star ingredient, which are then combined with a medley of complementary elements. The potatoes are typically cooked until fork-tender and then cooled before being mixed with a creamy dressing. The dressing, often made with mayonnaise, mustard, vinegar, and a touch of sugar, lends a delightful balance of richness and tanginess to the salad. Additional ingredients such as hard-boiled eggs, celery, pickles, and red onions are commonly incorporated, providing a pleasant crunch and enhancing the overall texture. The combination of flavors is further enhanced by a blend of seasonings, which may include salt, pepper, paprika, and fresh herbs like parsley or dill. This iconic Southern dish is a staple at backyard barbecues, potlucks, and family gatherings, evoking a sense of comfort and nostalgia with every creamy, flavorful bite.

Ingredients

  • 4 potatoes
  • 4 large eggs
  • ½ cup mayonnaise
  • ½ stalk celery, chopped
  • ¼ cup sweet relish
  • 1 clove garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons prepared mustard
  • salt and pepper to taste

Steps

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil. Add potatoes and cook until tender but still firm, about 15 minutes; drain and chop.
  3. At the same time, place eggs in a saucepan and cover with cold water. Bring water to a boil; cover, remove from heat, and let eggs stand in hot water for 10 to 12 minutes. Remove from hot water; peel and chop.
  4. Combine cooked potatoes and eggs with mayonnaise, celery, relish, garlic, mustard, salt, and pepper in a large bowl.
  5. Gently mix together and serve warm.
